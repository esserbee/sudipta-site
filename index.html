About

I work at the intersection of condensed matter theory, computational electromagnetics, and device physics, with a particular interest in two-dimensional materials and their applications in nanophotonic and optoelectronic systems.

I completed my PhD in Electrical Engineering at the University of Minnesota under the supervision of Prof. Tony Low. My graduate research focused on theoretical and numerical studies of novel two-dimensional (2D) materials and devices, including their optical, electronic, and plasmonic properties.

Earlier, I obtained my BSc in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). During my PhD, I was awarded the 3M Science and Technology doctoral fellowship and worked as a graduate research and teaching assistant.

Beyond academia, I completed R&D internships at 3M in 2018 and 2019, where I contributed to materials and device innovation projects. I am also interested in machine learning and AI, especially their application to nanophotonics and materials modeling.

Outside of research, I enjoy racket sports such as tennis, badminton, and pickleball, as well as soccer, hiking, camping, and playing instruments including ukulele, violin, and kalimba.
