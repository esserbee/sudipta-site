About Me

Bridging academic research and industry innovation in optical systems and semiconductor devices.

I'm a Hardware Engineer at Meta, where I work on sensor validation for optical systems including image sensors and ambient light sensors. My work spans the full lifecycle from design validation to production testing, ensuring world-class performance in consumer devices.

Previously, I contributed to display and optical engineering at Apple and LED innovation at Lumileds, bringing deep expertise in photonics and semiconductor physics to cutting-edge product development.

I hold a PhD in Electrical Engineering from the University of Minnesota, where I worked under Prof. Tony Low on theoretical and computational studies of two-dimensional materials and nanophotonic devices. My research has been published in top journals including Nature Communications and Physical Review, with over 450 citations.

I received my BSc from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) and was awarded the prestigious 3M Science and Technology Doctoral Fellowship during my graduate studies.

When I'm not working on sensors or simulations, you'll find me playing tennis, hiking trails, or experimenting with ukulele and violin.

Research

My academic work spans graphene plasmonics, 2D materials, metasurfaces, and nonlinear optics. Below are selected projects with links to publications.

Gas Identification with Graphene Plasmons

Demonstrated that graphene surface plasmons can selectively identify gas molecules through their unique infrared fingerprints, enabling highly sensitive and selective gas sensing without chemical functionalization.

257 citations · Nature Communications

View Publication

Tunable Graphene Metasurface Reflectarray

Designed electrically tunable graphene-based metasurfaces capable of dynamic beam steering, electromagnetic cloaking, and optical illusions through programmable phase control.

143 citations · Physical Review B

View Publication

Plasmonic Gas Sensing with Graphene Nanoribbons

Explored localized plasmon resonances in graphene nanoribbons for enhanced molecular detection, achieving high sensitivity through near-field concentration effects.

44 citations

View Publication

Nonlinear Optics in 2D Materials via Band Nesting

Discovered that band nesting in layered materials enables double-resonant second harmonic generation, opening pathways for tunable nonlinear optical devices and potential metasurface applications.

Physical Review B

View Publication

Graphene Plasmonic Metasurface for Beam Forming

Developed theoretical frameworks for graphene-based plasmonic metasurfaces enabling reconfigurable beam forming and integrated gas sensing functionalities.

III-V Quantum Well MOSFET Modeling

Performed quantum ballistic simulations of InGaAs/InAs quantum well MOSFETs, analyzing transport characteristics and capacitance-voltage behavior for next-generation transistor design.
